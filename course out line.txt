*PROJECT MANAGEMENT*

Course Overview

Project management encompasses the initiation, planning, execution, control, monitoring, and closure of a team's work to achieve defined goals and fulfill specific success criteria within a designated timeframe.

Project management is characterized by distinct and significant constraints related to scope, time, and cost. This trio is commonly referred to as the triple constraint of project management. The primary challenge faced by project managers is to accomplish project goals and objectives while adhering to these constraints. Consequently, effective planning and organization are crucial in establishing a pathway for a project to advance and be completed successfully.

Project management extends beyond merely scheduling tasks and assignments within the allocated timelines and budgets. A vital component of project management is the development and maintenance of positive relationships with all stakeholders involved in or contributing to various aspects of the project.

Successfully managing the expectations and partnerships of diverse stakeholders, including business partners, team members, and management, is essential for a project's success. Therefore, project management encompasses numerous facets and skills that must work in harmony to ensure that a project is completed successfully within the established timelines, budgets, and objectives.

This course will equip you with a comprehensive understanding and knowledge of project management, enabling you to effectively plan and manage projects for your organization.

The confidence and competence acquired through this course will also assist you in overcoming challenges and managing projects within the specified timelines and budgets, allowing you to establish objective performance measures for the project, thereby demonstrating your credibility and paving the way for further growth and advancement.

Course Objectives

The primary aim of this course is to equip participants with;

the essential skill set and confidence necessary to complete projects within the designated timelines and budgets, fulfilling all objectives established for the project.

the necessary perspective and comprehension to create objective measures and metrics to evaluate project progress and performance.

the requisite confidence and experience to align project goals with organizational objectives, thereby contributing to the growth and development of the organization.

the ability to formulate a project management framework suitable for both small- and large-scale projects within the organization.

the skills and experience to foster positive relationships with all relevant stakeholders and to define and allocate roles and responsibilities effectively.

the capability and expertise to prepare and review a project charter prior to the commencement of a project.

the experience and knowledge to identify and assess risks, addressing them proactively to prevent any hindrance to project success.

the ability to manage conflicts, constraints, and other challenges efficiently, thereby showcasing skill and potential and enhancing opportunities for growth and advancement.

An organization can only fulfill its goals through the effective use of human effort. Consequently, its 'human resource' is of utmost importance. The 'human resource' of an organization consists of the collective contributions in terms of efforts, skills, or capabilities of its workforce. To manage the diverse range of individuals within an organization, it is essential to implement strategies and systems that ensure optimal performance.

Human Resources Management represents a strategic method for effectively overseeing employees at all levels within an organization. It is structured to enhance employee performance, enabling the organization to secure a competitive edge in the marketplace. This approach emphasizes the processes of hiring, managing, releasing or replacing staff, and formulating policies to uphold decorum while retaining employees. It serves as a link between employee performance and the objectives of the organization.
